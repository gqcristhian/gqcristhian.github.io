About Me

I am a Mexican PhD graduated from UT-Dallas working in cosmology and large scale structure. I was born in Culiac√°n where I got my bachelor degree. I was selected to conduct a summer research program co-funded by the U.S. embassy in Mexico at UT-Dallas, where I later on decided to pursue my PhD in physics with support from the Mexican government through a CONACYT-Scholarship.

At the beginning of my PhD, my initial work at UT-Dallas was related to modified gravity and consistency/inconsitency of cosmological data. While investigating both topics, we were able to write several papers.

Additionally, we upgraded the version of the ISiTGR software package to test deviations from the standard cosmological model using phenomenological modified gravity parameters. We made the ISiTGR code publicly available and it is currently integrated into the Core-Cosmology-Library (CCL) used by the LSST-DESC collaboration, and DESILike used by the Dark-Energy-Spectroscopic-Instrument (DESI) collaboration.

Currently, I continue my research on modified gravity but I am also involved with the Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (BAO) analysis and the cosmological parameter estimation team in DESI. Additionally, I am involved with efforts cross-correlating DESI with photometric surveys that overlap the DESI footprint to perform 3x2-pt analysis. I will continue my research as an Einstein fellow at Harvard University in the fall.
