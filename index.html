About me

I am a Mexican PhD student at UT-Dallas working in cosmology and large scale structure. I born in Culiac√°n where I got my bachelor degree. I was awarded by a summer research program co-funded by the U.S. embassy in Mexico for an internship at UT-Dallas, where I later on decided to pursue my PhD in physics with support from the Mexican government through a CONACYT-Scholarship.

At the beginning of my PhD, my initial work at UT-Dallas was related to modified gravity and consistency/inconsitency of cosmological data. While investigating both topics, we were able to produce several papers.

Additionally, we upgraded the version of the ISiTGR software package to test deviations from the standard cosmological model using phenomenological modified gravity parameters. We made the ISiTGR code publicly available and we also used it to validate some modules of the Core-Cosmology-Library (CCL) code developed for the LSST-DESC collaboration, in which ISiTGR is now wrapped.

Currently, I keep my research on modified gravity but I am also heavily involved in the Dark-Energy-Spectroscopic-Instrument (DESI). In DESI, I have worked on performing BAO analysis, including two-point measurements, reconstruction, and BAO fitting. I am also involved in the cosmological parameter estimation team. Additionally, I am involved with cross-correlating DESI with photometric surveys to perform 3x2-pt analysis.

Research interests

  • Cosmological probes for dark energy

    I am interesting in exploring different probes for dark energy as well as exploring different dark energy models and data-driven tests.

  • Modified Gravity

    I am interested in testing general relativity using cosmological data. I have focused my research on testing GR using phenomenological parameterizations of modified gravity and extending these ones to model-independent tests of gravity. I plan to continue on this research and extending it to smaller scales.

  • Statistical inference and cosmological tensions

    I am also quite interested in learning data analysis tools and statistical inference for parameter estimation. I have also explore grounds on cosmological tension metrics and consistency/inconsistency tests.

  • Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (BAO)

    My research also has focused on performing clustering measurements as well as BAO analysis and detection. I am interested in looking forward to improving BAO modelling and its detection.

Codes

  • Integrated Software in Testing General Relativity (ISiTGR)

    The ISiTGR code is a publicly available package for testing general relativity using phenomenological modified gravity parameters. The package includes several modifications to CAMB to include modified gravity parameters. The code can assume a either functional form or use binning methods to perform tests of gravity.

    I also developed a python-to-fortran wrapper to ease the implementation of ISiTGR into external packages. I am glad to say that currently, ISiTGR is implemented into new generation packages that will look and test for deviations from general relativity, such as CCL and desilike, and CosmoSIS (under request). ISiTGR was a code initially developed by Dr. Mustapha Ishak and Dr. Jason Dossett, and later on I had the oportunity to continue its development.

  • BAO-Fitter

    The BAO-Fitter code was a package developed by me that relies on the old nbodykit and emcee to perform modelling of the BAO feature and BAO parameter constraints in Fourier space.

    I used BAO-Fitter to test the theoretical modelling of the BAO using DESI mocks. This was part of a previous mock challenge effort.
